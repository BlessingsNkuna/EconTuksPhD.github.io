The impact of renewable energy consumption to economic growth: A panel data application




Author: Roula Inglesi-Lotz
Year: 2016
Supervisor:
Email:

Abstract


Internationally, the importance of renewable energy in the energy mix has been increasingly appreciated. The advantages of the renewable energy usage for the world's energy security and the environment are indisputable and much discussed in the literature. However, its effects on the economic welfare of the countries are yet to be examined fully and described quantitatively. The purpose of this paper is to estimate the impact of the renewable energy consumption to economic welfare by employing panel data techniques. The results show that the influence of renewable energy consumption or its share to the total energy mix to economic growth is positive and statistically significant. From a policy point of view, promoting renewable energies bears benefits not only for the environment but also for the economic conditions of the countries.Read Full Paper (PDF)


Local municipality productive efficiency and its determinants in South Africa




Author: Nara Monkam
Year: 2014
Supervisor:
Email:

Abstract


This paper assesses the technical efficiency of 231 local municipalities in South Africa for 2007 and investigates the potential determinants of efficiency gaps using the non-parametric data envelopment analysis technique. Efficiency scores are explained in a second-stage regression model using a Tobit regression model. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first attempt, using such a technique, to assess technical efficiency at the local government level in the African context. The results show that, on average, B1 and B3 municipalities could have theoretically achieved the same level of basic services with about 16% and 80% fewer resources respectively. Furthermore, fiscal autonomy and the number and skill levels of the top management of a municipalityâ€™s administration were found to influence the productive efficiency of municipalities in South Africa. Perhaps most importantly, the results depict a bleak picture of the democratic behaviour at the local level in South Africa. It appears that higher income and highly educated households do not feel the incentive to be active participants in public decision-making processes. The Economic Impact of Climate Change




Author: Jane Smith
Year: 2023
Supervisor:
Email:

Abstract


This study explores the intersection of sustainability practices, emerging technologies, and human behavior in the context of rapidly shifting socio-economic landscapes. By integrating qualitative and quantitative methodologies, we examine the nuanced impacts of green technologies on consumption patterns, particularly in urban and semi-urban settings. The findings highlight critical barriers to adoption, including socio-cultural inertia, economic disparities, and policy misalignments, while identifying key leverage points for change. Through this interdisciplinary approach, we propose a novel framework to enhance policy design and implementation, aiming for equitable and scalable solutions to contemporary global challenges. This research contributes to a deeper understanding of the systemic changes required to achieve sustainable development goals and underscores the importance of aligning technological innovation with behavioral insights. Future directions include longitudinal studies to validate these findings across diverse geographies and economies.
